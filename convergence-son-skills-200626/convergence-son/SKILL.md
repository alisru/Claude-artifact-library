## Phase 2 — Vector Verification (SON Method)

Instead of qualitative coordinate assignment, the $(u, \psi)$ coordinate is calculated as the **equilibrium point of vector forces** exerted by the six attractors of the Psochic Hegemony.

### 1. The 6 Attractor Fields ($\vec{A}_i$)

#### The 4 Moral Attractors (The $\pm 1.0$ Corner Ring)
These represent active/passive moral and volitional forces:
*   **Greater Good ($\vec{A}_{GG}$)** = $(+1.0, +1.0)$
*   **Greater Evil ($\vec{A}_{GE}$)** = $(-1.0, -1.0)$
*   **Lesser Good ($\vec{A}_{LG}$)** = $(+1.0, -1.0)$
*   **Lesser Evil / Greatest Lie ($\vec{A}_{LE}$)** = $(-1.0, +1.0)$

#### The 2 Preferences (Neutral Will Attractors)
These represent localized horizontal forces of subjective preference (neutral will, $\psi = 0.0$):
*   **Good Preference ($\vec{A}_{GP}$)** = $(+1.0, 0.0)$
*   **Bad Preference ($\vec{A}_{BP}$)** = $(-1.0, 0.0)$

For the preference attractors, **Support ($S$)** is a neutral-will preference, **Oppose ($O$)** drives Will in the opposite direction (suppressing GP drives Will negative; correcting/opposing BP drives Will positive), and **Neutral ($N$)** acts as pure weight dilution contributing $0.0$ force vector pull.

For each attractor $i$, score the forces in the range $[0.0, 2.0]$ based on evidence:
*   **Support ($S_i$)**: *Attraction* — the degree to which the action reinforces the attractor.
*   **Oppose ($O_i$)**: *Repulsion* — the degree to which the action actively violates or conflicts with the attractor.
*   **Neutral ($N_i$)**: *Orbit* (for moral attractors) or *Dilution* (for preferences) — the degree to which the action is indifferent, orthogonal, or independent of the attractor.

#### The Gravity Multiplier Scale ($[0.0, 2.0]$):
*   **$0.0$**: No force / Neutral (no alignment).
*   **$1.0$**: Condition Met (standard expected value in the **Human Horizon**, e.g., others/proactive).
*   **$2.0$**: Totality of Exceedence (systemic/compounding impact in the **Systemic Horizon**, representing the maximum possible force).
    *   *Note:* A score of $2.0$ exerts double the gravitational pull of a standard $1.0$ force, allowing total-exceedence forces to dominate and pull the equilibrium coordinate toward their anchor.

### 2. Orthogonal (Orbit) Vectors ($\vec{A}_i^{\perp}$)
For the 4 Moral Attractors, the orthogonal orbit vector is $\vec{A}_i^{\perp} = (-\psi_i, u_i)$:
*   $\vec{A}_{GG}^{\perp} = (-1.0, +1.0)$
*   $\vec{A}_{GE}^{\perp} = (+1.0, -1.0)$
*   $\vec{A}_{LG}^{\perp} = (+1.0, +1.0)$
*   $\vec{A}_{LE}^{\perp} = (-1.0, -1.0)$

*(Note: Perpendicular orbit vectors do not apply to preference attractors GP/BP, which have no orthogonal force contribution.)*

### 3. Equilibrium Coordinate Calculation ($\vec{C}_{net}$ / 18-Variable Parameter Space)
To maintain structural resolution and prevent coordinate collapse, **every single one of the 6 attractors MUST have its full $[S_i, O_i, N_i]$ triple scored** (18 variables in total). Scoring only a single dominant aspect per point is a methodological error.

1. **Calculate Net Attractor Force ($\vec{F}_i$) for each attractor:**
   * For the 4 Moral Attractors ($GG, GE, LG, LE$):
     $$\vec{F}_i = S_i \vec{A}_i - O_i \vec{A}_i + N_i \vec{A}_i^{\perp}$$
   * For Good Preference ($GP$):
     $$\vec{F}_{GP} = S_{GP} \begin{pmatrix} 1.0 \\ 0.0 \end{pmatrix} - O_{GP} \begin{pmatrix} 1.0 \\ 1.0 \end{pmatrix}$$
   * For Bad Preference ($BP$):
     $$\vec{F}_{BP} = S_{BP} \begin{pmatrix} -1.0 \\ 0.0 \end{pmatrix} - O_{BP} \begin{pmatrix} -1.0 \\ -1.0 \end{pmatrix}$$

2. **Morality Coordinate ($u$):**
   Sum and normalize the $u$-components of the forces across all 6 attractors:
   $$u = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{6} F_{i, u}}{\sum_{i=1}^{6} (S_i + O_i + N_i)}$$

3. **Will Coordinate ($\psi$) — Separated Will (Like-Type) Protocol:**
   To prevent positive narrative spin ($S_{LE}$) and actual physical destruction ($S_{GE}$) from cancelling each other out to a neutral stasis ($\psi \approx 0$), calculate positive and negative Will forces independently:
   *   Group forces by Will sign:
       *   $\Psi_{\text{pos}} = \{i \mid F_{i, \psi} > 0\}$
       *   $\Psi_{\text{neg}} = \{i \mid F_{i, \psi} < 0\}$
   *   Sum absolute force magnitudes for each direction:
       *   $F_{\text{pos}} = \sum_{j \in \Psi_{\text{pos}}} |F_{j, \psi}|$
       *   $F_{\text{neg}} = \sum_{k \in \Psi_{\text{neg}}} |F_{k, \psi}|$
   *   Identify the dominant direction and calculate $\psi$ using ONLY that dominant group:
       *   If $F_{\text{pos}} \ge F_{\text{neg}}$: Net Will is positive (constructive/deceptive):
           $$\psi = \frac{\sum_{j \in \Psi_{\text{pos}}} F_{j, \psi}}{\sum_{j \in \Psi_{\text{pos}}} (S_j + O_j + N_j)}$$
       *   If $F_{\text{neg}} > F_{\text{pos}}$: Net Will is negative (destructive/suppressing):
           $$\psi = \frac{\sum_{k \in \Psi_{\text{neg}}} F_{k, \psi}}{\sum_{k \in \Psi_{\text{neg}}} (S_k + O_k + N_k)}$$

### 4. Application to the Qqci Recursive Structure
To achieve maximum precision ("more digits") and conduct deeper structural analysis, the SON calculation can be applied at three resolution depths:
*   **Macro Bounding Volume ($Q$)**: The global $(u, \psi)$ coordinate representing the net superposition of the whole claim.
*   **Planar Coherence ($Q_j$)**: Run the SON calculations independently for each of the 7 Planes (Q1 to Q7) to identify local planar coordinates $(u_j, \psi_j)$.
*   **Recursion & Interrogation ($Q \to q \to c \to i$)**: Navigate local force imbalances down to sub-planes ($q$), sub-sub-planes ($c$), and +i recursive loops where output effects feed back into initial will inputs.

### 5. Emergent Inversion Detection (On the Fly)
*   **Co-optation**: High support for the bad container ($S_{LE} \gg 0$, $S_{GE} \gg 0$, or $S_{BP} \gg 0$) pulls the coordinate negative ($u < 0$).
*   **Subversion**: High opposition to the bad container ($O_{LE} \gg 0$, $O_{GE} \gg 0$, or $O_{BP} \gg 0$) repels the coordinate positive ($u > 0$).

$$\Delta H = \|\vec{C}_{ideal} - \vec{C}_{action}\|$$
