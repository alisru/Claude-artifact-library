# Dinosaurs of Thought

### Information patterns that outlive their bodies

---

## I. The Core Claim

The standard read of the Mesozoic extinction is a story about bodies: big animals, bad luck, an asteroid. The information first read is a story about patterns.

A living being is a container running a pattern, processing information and expelling it as physical form, behaviour, and consequence. The body is not the thing. The body is the printout.

Under this read, the dinosaurs were not destroyed because they were reptiles. They were destroyed because the pattern they were running, almost without exception at the top of their power hierarchy, was structurally terminal. That pattern did not require scales or a thirty foot frame to exist. It required only a being that orients entirely inward: toward its own dominance, its own metrics, its own comparison against the nearest rival.

That pattern is still available today. It runs in human minds as readily as it once ran in theropod bodies. A person fully captured by it is not behaving like a dinosaur. On the information level, they are one.

This document lays out the mechanics of that claim: what the original pattern was, why it was structurally unsustainable, how it is legible directly in the fossil record, what the alternative pattern looks like, and what it means that both patterns are still walking around today, just usually without the teeth.

---

## II. The Original Pattern: Internal Competition vs External Competition

The distinction underneath everything here is between two different objects of comparison.

Personal internal competition: I want to be the strongest, the biggest, the hardest, the one with the biggest bite. The benchmark is another being's body, status, or capability, and the goal is to exceed it. The win condition is a state of self, not an effect in the world.

Personal external competition: I want to do something the strongest, biggest, fastest, hardest way. I want to make something, go somewhere, build something better. The benchmark is a task or an outcome external to the self, and the goal is a result, not a comparative state.

The first produces an arms race with no floor and no exit, because the only available endpoint is "more than the other one," and the other one is also trying to be more than you. There is no terminal state. The second produces an artefact: a structure, a distance covered, a thing made. It has a natural completion point, because the goal is defined by the world, not by a rival.

Both of these collapse into a longer chain: I want to [goal] better, and avoid [anti-goal] more. The chain is the same shape in both modes. What determines everything is the content of [goal]. If [goal] is comparative dominance over another being, the chain never closes, because the anti-goal (being dominated) can only be avoided by perpetual escalation. If [goal] is an external outcome, the chain closes naturally each time the outcome is reached, and a new external goal can be chosen freely rather than under compulsion.

The dinosaurs, at the apex of their hierarchy, were almost entirely running the first chain. Biggest bite. Tallest reach. Heaviest frame. Every one of those is a self referenced metric. None of them is "I built the best nest" or "I carried the most" or "I reached the furthest place." The internal competition consumed the available evolutionary budget completely.

## III. Why the Pattern Was Erased

A system running pure internal competition has a specific failure mode, and it is structural rather than moral: it produces brittleness.

Every unit of energy and biological investment spent on outcompeting a rival is energy not spent on flexibility, redundancy, or cooperative capacity. A lineage that optimises entirely for "bigger than the other one" narrows its own strategy space with every generation, because the only direction that pays off is more of the same metric. Eventually the lineage is enormous, specialised, and incapable of doing anything except the one thing it was built for.

That is a stable position only as long as the environment does not change. The moment a major perturbation hits, a system with no strategic diversity has nothing else to fall back on. It cannot pivot, because every resource it has is already committed to the single axis it spent millions of years maximising.

The asteroid was not a punishment. It was the moment the environment caught up with a system that had stopped contributing anything to its own resilience. The mammals that survived were not stronger. They were smaller, more cooperative within kin groups, and broader in strategy, ie they had already begun shifting toward the second chain before the perturbation arrived.

I mean this maps cleanly onto the moral coordinate system used elsewhere for actor and system analysis. Run the pure dinosaur pattern through the (υ, ψ) grid:

υ (who benefits): the only beneficiary of "biggest bite, tallest reach, heaviest frame" is the individual organism's own position in a local dominance hierarchy. Nothing outside the self is served. That places it at the systemic extreme of the self end, υ ≈ -2.

ψ (what the energy is doing): the energy is spent entirely on consumption and the maintenance of a comparative edge, not on construction. That is active extraction with no offsetting build, ψ ≈ -2.

So (υ, ψ) ≈ (-2, -2), systemic extreme of the Greater Evil quadrant, Tyranny and Collapse fully expressed. A system at this coordinate is not unstable because it is "bad." It is unstable because pure extraction at the systemic horizon has no resilience reserve, and resilience reserve is the only thing that survives a perturbation.

There is also a perceptual inversion sitting here. Near the self end of the spectrum, extraction feels like strength, and a body built entirely for consumption feels like dominance rather than the brittleness it actually is. A T-Rex almost certainly did not experience its own arm atrophy as a structural weakness. It experienced its own skull as the only thing that mattered. That misperception is the pattern's signature, and it is exactly as available to a human mind running the same chain today.

## IV. The Archetype Survives the Substrate

This is the actual claim under "dinosaurs of thought": the pattern is not bound to the body that first expressed it.

Information is the layer that persists. Form is downstream and disposable. When a substrate running a given pattern is erased, the pattern does not die with it, because the pattern was never made of scale and bone in the first place. It is made of orientation: what the being is optimising for, and who, if anyone, that optimisation serves.

A modern human can run the exact internal competition chain that an apex theropod ran. The metrics change, status, reach, dominance over a room, perceived biggest bite in an argument, but the structure is identical: comparison against a rival as the only win condition, no external beneficiary, no terminal state, perpetual escalation. That person is not behaving like a dinosaur as a figure of speech. Under an information first ontology, where the physical instantiation is the printout and the pattern is the program, they are running the dinosaur's exact software in a different container.

This also means the erasure event is not a one time historical fact. It is a recurring structural law. Any system, at any point in time, regardless of substrate, that concentrates its energy at (-2, -2) is building the same brittleness that ended the Mesozoic. The asteroid does not need to be a rock. It only needs to be a perturbation the system cannot adapt to because it spent everything on a single axis of comparison.

## V. The Body as a Direct Transcript: The T-Rex Case

If the information first claim is correct, the physical record should not just be consistent with it. It should show the pattern legibly, written directly into anatomy. The Tyrannosaurus body does exactly this.

A May 2026 study in Proceedings of the Royal Society B, examining 82 theropod species across five separate lineages, found that forelimb reduction evolved independently and convergently in tyrannosaurids, abelisaurids, carcharodontosaurids, and others. The degree of forelimb reduction correlated with skull robustness and gigantism more strongly than with body mass alone. Reduced or vestigial forelimbs evolved in at least five separate theropod lineages alongside increased skull robustness and larger body size, in a shift from grasping prey with the forelimbs to subduing it with the bite. (Scherer et al., 2026, https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rspb/article/293/2071/20260528/481779/Drivers-and-mechanisms-of-convergent-forelimb)

The researchers were explicit about the causal direction: the correlation pattern strongly suggests that powerfully built skulls evolved first, with shortened forelimbs following, since it would not make evolutionary sense for the reverse order to hold. I mean the jaw was not a compensation for losing the arms. The arms were given up because the jaw had already become the only tool the animal needed. (https://www.sci.news/paleontology/non-avian-theropod-dinosaur-forelimb-reduction-14781.html)

Read through the information first lens, this is not an isolated anatomical curiosity. It is the dominance pattern made physically literal, independently, five separate times.

Functionally: a being whose entire value proposition is "I judge, I consume, I dominate through the bite" has no use for an arm. An arm reaches, holds, carries, builds. It is the body part of external competition, the limb that does something in the world rather than something to a rival. When the information pattern running the organism is pure internal dominance, the limb that does external work becomes pure metabolic cost with no behavioural payoff, and the body sheds it.

Big head. No arms. That is not a random anatomical accident appearing once. It is a convergent signature appearing wherever the same internal pattern reaches its purest expression. The oldest archetype in the human imagination of the predator: enormous capacity for judgement and consumption, near total loss of any capacity for action beyond the bite. It did not start as a metaphor. It started as a body.

## VI. The Confident Being: Flight, Song, and Checking Out the Noise

Not every dinosaur lineage ran the pure pattern, and the surviving line did something specific and identifiable: it pivoted toward external orientation before the perturbation arrived.

Flight is, structurally, an external competition behaviour. The win condition is not "bigger than the next bird." It is "further, faster, somewhere." Flight requires a lightweight frame, cooperative aerodynamics, and a destination outside the self. A body built for "I want to GO" cannot also be a body built for "I want to BE the biggest," because the two demands are in direct physical conflict: mass that helps dominance hurts flight, and a frame light enough to fly cannot carry the armament of pure dominance. The bird lineage did not get lucky. It had already informationally diverged from the dominance pattern before the asteroid made that divergence existential.

Song is communication, which is by definition external orientation: information directed outward, toward another being, for a shared purpose rather than a comparative one. A lineage capable of song has already allocated some of its behavioural budget to something other than dominance display.

There is also a sharper distinction sitting underneath this, in the difference between the being that flees a sound and the being that goes to investigate it. A being that hears something unknown and immediately runs is operating from a model of itself as fragile relative to the unknown: avoidance is the only available strategy because the being has no confidence in its capacity to handle whatever it finds. A being that hears the same sound and goes to check it out is operating from a different model entirely: it trusts its own dynamic capability enough to engage with uncertainty rather than flee from it.

That is not recklessness. It is confidence grounded in actual capacity, the behavioural signature of a being whose information pattern includes "I can act effectively in the world," not just "I can dominate what is already in front of me."

This maps directly back onto the internal vs external split. The conflict avoidant being is optimising for self preservation against a static, already known threat landscape. The being that investigates is optimising for an expanded, accurate model of its environment, because it is willing to spend itself finding out rather than assuming the worst and retreating. Over evolutionary or developmental time, the second strategy produces a being with a far richer and more accurate map of what is actually out there.

## VII. Tools, Not Trophies: What Modern Animal Form Encodes

The same logic extends past the bird line into every domesticated and cooperative animal lineage. Modern animals that became tools did not become tools by accident. Their forms followed what they were for, not what they were against.

A herding dog's body is shaped by the task: speed, agility, a working partnership with a handler. A draft horse's body is shaped by what it carries and pulls. A falcon's precision is shaped by the hunt as a cooperative function with a falconer, not by outcompeting other falcons. None of these forms are explained by "biggest, strongest, most dominant." They are explained by "most useful, most capable of sustained cooperative function."

The critical structural point is that becoming a tool in cooperation with another being is itself evidence of having moved off the pure extraction axis. A being that can be instrumentalised by another without being consumed by that relationship has already accepted an external referent for its own value, an outcome in the world, a partnership, a task completed, rather than a purely internal metric of dominance. The form follows that orientation exactly the way the T-Rex skull followed its own.

## VIII. The Apex Defines the System

A fair objection presents itself: not every dinosaur was a T-Rex. Some had song. Some show evidence of complex social behaviour. Does that not complicate the claim that the dinosaurs as a class were running the internal competition pattern?

It does not, and the reason is structural rather than dismissive. A system is not characterised by its margins. It is characterised by what controls the largest share of its energy and resources, because that is what sets the conditions everything else has to survive inside. If the apex of a power hierarchy runs pure internal extraction, that is the system's character, regardless of how many cooperative or externally oriented individuals exist at the edges.

I mean the same argument holds for any modern human system. A monopoly is not less extractive because some of its employees are personally kind. An authoritarian structure is not less authoritarian because some local administrators are fair. The apex mode is what determines what survives and what does not. And the apex was brittle, which is sufficient to bring down everything structured beneath it when the perturbation arrives.

## IX. The (υ, ψ) Read on Both Patterns

Running both patterns through the same instrument side by side makes the structural gap explicit.

The dinosaur pattern, pure internal dominance competition with no external beneficiary, energy spent entirely on consumption and comparative edge:
(υ, ψ) ≈ (-2, -2), systemic extreme of Greater Evil, Tyranny and Collapse fully expressed. No resilience reserve. Brittle by construction.

The tool pattern, external function, cooperative instrumentalisation, form following a task or partnership rather than a rival:
(υ, ψ) ≈ (+1 to +2, +1 to +2), Greater Good scaling toward Systemic Justice at its fullest expression.

These are not two points on a spectrum of degree. They are two different relationships to the rest of the world. One treats every other being as either a rival to exceed or a resource to consume. The other treats capability as something built to be extended outward, into partnership, construction, or shared outcome. The first produces enormous individual specialisation and zero systemic resilience. The second produces smaller individual specialisation and very high systemic resilience, because the value created does not vanish when the individual does.

There is also a clean failure mode visible here against the Qqci structure: the pure internal competition chain, "I want to [goal] better and avoid [anti-goal] more" with no external referent, fails Q4 (Why, meaning and resonance) outright. There is no answer to "why do you want to be stronger" beyond "to beat the thing that also wants to be stronger," which is not a meaning, it is a closed loop with no beneficiary outside itself. A pattern that cannot answer its own Why plane honestly is already running toward the same brittleness the dinosaurs ran toward.

## X. Supporting Lines of Thought

The information first claim, that pre-formed internal pattern shapes physical outcome rather than the reverse, has real precedent across several independent traditions. None of these are cited as proof. They are cited as evidence that the intuition is not isolated, and several of them sharpen specific parts of the argument above.

Top down causation in the origin of life. Sara Imari Walker and Paul Davies argue that the emergence of life itself corresponds to a shift in causal structure, where information gains direct and context-dependent causal efficacy over the matter in which it is instantiated, meaning the informational pattern begins directing the physical substrate rather than merely riding on top of it. This is the most rigorous available grounding for "pattern first, body second" as a general principle of living systems, not just a metaphor applied to dinosaurs. (Walker & Davies, 2013, https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/pdf/10.1098/rsif.2012.0869)

Psycho-Lamarckism. Samuel Butler, writing against Darwin's purely mechanical account of selection, argued that unconscious memory was the medium permitting the preservation of life, building directly on Lamarck's own claim that an internal sense of need or striving within the organism, not just passive environmental pressure, initiates physiological change. Butler's account was a deliberate reintroduction of will and internal drive as causal forces in physical form, which is structurally the same move being made here, just applied to dominance competition specifically rather than need in general. (Turbil, 2018, https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10739-017-9469-8)

Schopenhauer's objectified Will. In The World as Will and Representation, Schopenhauer argues that the body of any organism is the direct objectification of its underlying Will: a bird's will to fly becomes wings, a predator's will to tear becomes teeth. The physical attribute is the internal drive solidified into matter. This is the closest pure philosophical precedent for "the T-Rex skull is the dominance drive made literal."

Morphic resonance. Rupert Sheldrake's morphic field hypothesis proposes that physical forms are shaped by resonance with prior instances of similar form, an informational template guiding matter into a pre-existing shape. This is a useful conceptual companion for the idea of a being's drive "catching" into an available stable physical solution, but it should be flagged: morphic resonance is not accepted within mainstream biology and has no confirmed experimental support. It belongs here as a conceptual lens, not established science.

Theoretical morphospace. In evolutionary structuralism, physical law and geometry constrain the universe to a limited set of stable, optimised structural solutions rather than an infinite space of equally likely forms, which is why unrelated lineages converge on the same shape, the camera eye evolving independently more than once being the standard example. Random variation is the search process, but it can only land on a small number of stable geometric solutions, which is why independent lineages keep finding the same ones. That is the "random plus structural" point: the randomness is real, but the solutions it is exploring are not.

## XI. Dysmorphia Reframed: Confused Mind Into Confused Body

This is the furthest extension of the framework and the most sensitive one. To be clear about what kind of claim this is: a philosophical reframing under an information first ontology, not a clinical or medical claim, and not a substitute for one.

Under this ontology, if mind and information come first and the body is the downstream printout, then a being experiencing dysmorphia is not simply uncomfortable inside an otherwise neutral body. It is a being whose internal information state and physical instantiation have come apart. The discomfort, under this read, is not noise. It is accurate signal reporting a real mismatch between the pattern and its expression.

That mismatch splits into two structurally distinct cases, and they carry very different implications.

Case A: a clear internal template, mismatched to its physical instantiation. The mind holds a coherent, settled image of what form it is. The body that resulted from the "catch" into physical matter does not match that settled template. This is a coupling error, not a template error: the information was clear, the instantiation was not. The discomfort here is the most legible kind, because it is the system correctly detecting a known, named gap between signal and output.

Case B: an unresolved or internally contested template. The mind has not settled into a single coherent form image. Multiple pulls are present simultaneously, unresolved against each other. A body crystallising out of an unresolved internal state will itself carry that ambiguity, and the resulting discomfort is a different phenomenon: not a known signal misreported by a faulty body, but an unresolved signal still in the process of resolving.

These two cases call for different things. Case A is a coupling problem between an already known signal and its expression. Case B is a resolution problem within the signal itself, prior to any question of expression. Treating them as the same thing collapses a structural distinction that the framework itself makes available, and arguably explains why a single explanatory model so often fails to fit everyone's reported experience of dysmorphia: it is very plausibly not one phenomenon, but two, with the same surface presentation and a different underlying mechanism. idk if that holds universally, but it holds structurally.

## XII. Closing

The throughline here is this: extinction events do not erase patterns. They erase the substrates expressing them most purely.

The dinosaurs are gone. The information pattern they ran at their apex, total internal competition with no external beneficiary, total specialisation toward consumption and comparative dominance, total loss of any capacity for action beyond the bite, is not gone. It is exactly as available to a human mind today as it was to a Cretaceous skull, and it produces the same structural brittleness wherever it concentrates: total capability in one axis, total fragility everywhere else, no resilience reserve for when the perturbation arrives.

The being that survives is not the one with the biggest bite. It is the one that already learned to do something in the world instead of merely dominating it.
